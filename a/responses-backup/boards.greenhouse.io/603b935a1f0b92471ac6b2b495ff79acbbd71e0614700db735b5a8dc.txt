    <p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bloomreach is the leader in commerce experience™. Our flagship product, brX, is the only digital experience platform that utilizes the full spectrum of commerce data, from product to customer, alongside content management capabilities, and AI-driven search, merchandising, and personalization in one flexible, API-first platform. We serve over 700 global brands including Albertsons, Staples, Bosch, Puma, FC Bayern München, and Marks &amp; Spencer, and power $200 billion in digital commerce experiences annually.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our mission for our team is to make careers at Bloomreach the most impactful professional experience of people’s lives. Bloomreach is committed to a diverse and inclusive workplace. At Bloomreach we have created a safe space and actively encourage Bloomreachers to bring their whole selves to work, embracing what makes each person in our company unique.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For more information, visit </span><a href="https://c212.net/c/link/?t=0&amp;l=en&amp;o=3034059-1&amp;h=1169324551&amp;u=https%3A%2F%2Fbit.ly%2F2EfrSOZ&amp;a=Bloomreach.com"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bloomreach.com</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, follow us on Twitter </span><a href="https://c212.net/c/link/?t=0&amp;l=en&amp;o=3034059-1&amp;h=657607192&amp;u=https%3A%2F%2Ftwitter.com%2Fbloomreach_tm%3Flang%3Del&amp;a=%40Bloomreach_tm"><span style="font-weight: 400;">@Bloomreach_tm</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, or on LinkedIn at </span><a href="https://c212.net/c/link/?t=0&amp;l=en&amp;o=3034059-1&amp;h=2476920404&amp;u=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.linkedin.com%2Fcompany%2Fbloomreach&amp;a=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.linkedin.com%2Fcompany%2Fbloomreach"><span style="font-weight: 400;">http://www.linkedin.com/company/bloomreach</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Do you love frontend development and are you good at it? Would you like to build a large-scale &amp; fast evolving app using Angular &amp; TypeScript? Would you like to talk about why we might be the best team for you to join right now?? Curious? Read on! <br>(Your s</span><span style="font-weight: 400;">alary start from&nbsp;</span><strong>2700€ per month</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> with stock options and other benefits included. Working in one of our </span><strong>Central Europe offices or from home</strong><span style="font-weight: 400;"> on a </span><strong>full-time basis.)</strong></p>

<h2>About your role and the team:</h2>
</div>
</div>
<div class="section global-width">
<div class="block">

<div class="section global-width">

<div class="block-wrapper description">
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We are a team of eight people at the moment. We cooperate tightly as a single unit on a multitude of tasks and challenges in order to make our application the best to serve our customers’ needs. Since not all of us enjoy tasks with a focus on styling, a subteam of stylers has been formed that takes care of our UI library of low-level components.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We are facing a variety of tasks on our daily basis that fall mostly into three categories - designing and developing new features,&nbsp; maintaining existing features in the underlying codebase and sometimes prototyping new features as POCs.</span></p>
<h2>What we expect of the candidate:</h2>
<p><strong>Must have</strong></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">advanced TypeScript (or JavaScript with a strong will to switch to TypeScript)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">advanced Angular (or similar component-based framework with a strong will to switch to Angular)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">experience with software design &amp; architecture (be able to propose and implement an effective &amp; efficient solution based on problem definition without detailed instructions)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The ability to work in project teams effectively, being reliable and communicating clearly.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A “can-do” attitude</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Should have</strong></span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">experience with developing bigger projects</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">At least an intermediate skill with scss/less/css (be able to get things done in reasonable quality if your styler colleagues are busy)</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Preferably have</strong></span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">experience with testing (Karma, Jasmine, Cypress)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">experience with RxJS</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Nice have</strong></span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">experience with mentoring less experienced colleagues</span></li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<h2>How we work:</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our entire engineering team works in 6 week cycles, consisting of a specification week, 4 development weeks and a cooldown week. Each developer is assigned to one or more projects during this cycle and aims to deliver the project together with other project team members from various other teams. In addition to working on projects, we also focus on other tasks - not limited to working on our backlog, providing an L3 support to our client facing colleagues or making improvements to our product through an initiative called “Happy consultants”.<br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">In order to keep our high quality standards, each change in code we do gets reviewed and our automated pipeline builds these changes, runs a series of tests, runs the linter, packages the outputs and deploys them onto a development environment.<br></span><span style="font-weight: 400;">We are a team of diverse skill sets - you will need to share your experience and knowledge (during code reviews and ideally also beyond) with other colleagues and help them grow just like we all will help and support you from the minute you join us.</span></p>
<h2>Challenges:</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Here are some of the challenges that kept us busy in the past:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Micro frontend research</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our application is split up into modules but we are experimenting with the idea of loosening up the coupling even a bit more and splitting our large application into a collection of smaller ones run under a single container application.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Identify the pros and cons of this approach and what problems will it solve effectively and what other problems it might bring.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Take into account how this switch potentially affects not the architecture alone but also the execution, deployment and DX.</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Optimizing build performance</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The larger an application gets, the more complex the build becomes. Our application consists of hundreds of components, directives, services, pipes and other functions.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Find a way to optimise the build in order to make the DX and the pipeline build performance better.</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Optimizing change detection</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our application aims to deliver a swift interaction experience to its users without the feeling that something is lagging.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Identify components that are underperforming.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Analyze their bottlenecks using the profiler.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Optimize the runtime performance of the problematic code parts.</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Data visualisation</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Our real-time analyses like trends, funnels, reports, and segmentations allow users to gain insights about their data from multiple perspectives. We integrate with external data sources spanning multiple relational databases and big data storage systems.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Build an interface for users to query data from data sources located outside of Exponea to build the basis for our analyses and visualizations.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Create complex data visualizations using the Highcharts library or similar suitable tool.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Be proactive in proposing solutions which will help users to better understand their data.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Improve test quality and extend test coverage.</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Extend UI library</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">We have created a mature UI library with the goal in mind to unify the look, behavior, and the API of our reusable components. This library already consists of a solid foundation of components but the innovation in Exponea goes hand in hand with the need to create new components and enhance existing ones.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Create new reusable components while focusing on clear API, stability, best possible UX and modern browser support.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Test your component well. Use unit tests to cover all thinkable and unthinkable scenarios your component may go through to make it robust.</span></li>
</ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Other than that…</span></li>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">We work hard to have sustainable code, but we still have some code in our codebase, especially from the early startup era, that was written in haste to keep the business running - you will need to be able to get around in complex code and help us refactor it.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Automated testing of our code is important to us. You will need to cover your code, help us improve existing test quality and extend overall test coverage - spanning from unit tests, through integration tests to automated e2e tests.</span></li>
</ul>
</ul>
